# RevUP Intake — Design Context

## Users
Small business owners, solopreneurs, coaches, consultants who feel "something is off" in their work. Filling this out late evening / weekend after a long week. Likely on laptop, possibly phone. Skeptical of agencies, burnt by bad consultants, allergic to predatory marketing. Want to feel met where they are, not pitched at.

## Brand Personality
**Three words:** calm, opinionated, fiduciary.
- **Voice**: editorial — like a thoughtful long-form magazine, not a SaaS form. Speaks plainly, refuses gracefully, explains the why.
- **Emotional goals**: relief (after years of cortisol marketing), recognition (someone who works the way you wish more agencies did), trust (built through restraint, not claims).
- Anti-cortisol. Anti-manufactured-urgency. Anti-predatory-tactics. Sovereignty for the founder AND their audience.

## Aesthetic Direction
**Theme**: dark — chosen because the user is filling this out at 9pm on a Sunday, in their study, after a draining week. Calm warm charcoal, never pure black. Reduces stress, signals seriousness without aggression.

**Existing visual language** (intentional and working):
- Warm charcoal palette (#141614 base, ascending surface tones)
- Cormorant Garamond serif for emphasis + headlines (editorial gravitas)
- Inter sans for body
- Caveat hand-script for "why we ask" eyebrow + occasional handwritten flourishes
- JetBrains Mono for eyebrow labels + tiny meta
- Sage green accent in oklch (0.72 0.09 148) — calm, never neon
- Gold reserved for brand mark + tiny geometry only

**References**: Tufte editorial design, Klim type foundry, hand-bound notebooks, museum exhibit captions.
**Anti-references**: SaaS dashboards, agency landing pages, anything in the "AI cyberpunk" lineage, neon gradients, glass cards, big rounded-icon-above-heading patterns.

## Design Principles
1. **Restraint as signal.** Every refused tactic (no fake urgency, no inflated CTAs, no manufactured scarcity) is itself a brand statement. The form's quietness is the message.
2. **Editorial over operational.** Read more like a thoughtful magazine than a form. Generous whitespace. Cormorant for moments that matter. Margin annotations as a structural pattern, not decoration.
3. **Educate-don't-force.** Where copy could declare ("we don't do X") it instead explains ("X produces these consequences, here's why we work this way") — leaves sovereignty with the reader.
4. **Calm motion.** Subtle entrance fades, soft transitions, never bouncy or springy. Reduced-motion fully respected.
5. **Hand-drawn warmth, not handcrafted preciousness.** Caveat for marginalia. SVG plant metaphor. Rough textures and slightly imperfect lines OK; overdone "artisanal" feel not OK.

## Notes for the impeccable framework
- Cormorant Garamond and Inter are on the skill's reflex-fonts-to-reject list. Both are deliberate brand choices and not under reconsideration in this pass — surface alternatives only if there's a strong distinctive reason, otherwise keep.
- The why-we-ask sidebar uses `border-left: 1.5px` as a structural channel divider (not a generic alert stripe). The skill's BAN 1 categorically bans border-left >1px; reframe is fine if there's a more distinctive treatment but the structural intent must remain.
